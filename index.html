Why China?

Gradually, China is heading toward a technologically advanced future as it’s currently the world’s second largest economy. Being one of the world’s most innovative nations, it is also a place of large political and societal interest as its migrant population increases at a rate of over a hundred thousand every year. Understandably so, as China is one of the world’s most populous countries with a heaping 18% of the world’s population living there. It is also one of the world's biggest countries with an amazingly interesting history and a rich culture with fascinating traditions. Its 9.6 million square kilometre surface area covers a whopping five geographical time zones and borders 14 countries.

This delightfully big land is filled with famous sights and charming friendly citizens. The people of China proudly celebrate their engaging history and traditions through many different festivals and holidays, famously the Spring Festival and Chinese New Year, also known as the Lunar festival. Not to mention the huge variety of delicious food and ancient medicine that around 65.7 million tourists travel from all around the world to China for. And variety is something you get in every sense of the word in China, as it also applies to their climate, their sights and their people, who are all part of the foundation of one of the world’s fourth most travelled countries.

Best Tourist Attractions in China

The Great Wall of China is the world's longest structure, the world's largest military defense structure and one of the world's most visited tourist attractions. Since 1987, another interesting fact is that the Great Wall of China has been listed as a World Heritage Site.

The Forbidden City is the imperial palace area in Beijing. It was built during the Ming Dynasty when the capital was moved from Nanjing to Beijing. The Forbidden City was completed in 1420. The area consists of two parts. The emperor and his family lived in the northern part. Ceremonies and the emperor's audiences were held in the southern part.

Terracotta Army is an archaeological find of about 8,000 full-scale clay models of soldiers and horses placed in a military formation. The find, which is part of Emperor Qin Shi Huangdi's mausoleum, is located in the Lintong district about 30 kilometers east of central Xi'an in China's Shaanxi Province and is located about two kilometers east of the tomb of the country's first emperor Qin Shi Huangdi.

Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding is a breeding facility that works for the cause of saving giant pandas instead of earning money. The research base started off with 6 pandas they saved in 2008, and had over 120 panda births since then. According to the facility their goal is "be a world-class research facility, conservation education center, and international educational tourism destination.".

Traditions / Festivals

The Spring Festival is a festival that begins on the first day in the first month of the lunar calendar and ends on the 15th with a festival where the residents will hang lanterns on their door and set off firecrackers, it is their type of new year celebration. In preparation for this the Chinese people start by cleaning their houses, shopping, cooking and more. One big reason as to why it is so important to them is that they get to meet close ones who live far away. When they meet, the young ones get to show off their new clothes and give best wishes and a toast to the elders' health and happiness and in return the young ones will be rewarded money. To understand it better, the Spring Festival to the Chinese people is what Christmas is to us. There are however a few certain things to avoid if you do not want bad luck, like certain words and behaviors.

The Lunar Festival is the big festival that begins on the 15th and marks the end of the Spring Festival. There will be fireworks and beautiful lanterns that come in many shapes and forms. Within lanterns there can be riddles that if you solve you may get a prize, this is an important part of the festival and many people participate in the festival for this. If you have watched any chinese movie there is a big chance you have seen people dancing in lion suits, this is a really common activity and is also a big tourist attraction. There are many folk dances like the Lion’s Dance and Walking on the Stilts. The performers dance to loud music and impersonate animals, clowns, monks and more. They also commonly eat Yuan Xiao which is a sweet ball dumpling made of glutinous.

Culture

China is a multi religious country. Taoism, Islam, Buddhism, Protestantism, and Catholicism are religoins that have grown into culture-shaping neigborhoods. China's culture is one of the oldest cultures in the world.A crucial part of Chinese culture includes many values that are rooted in spiritual values such as harmony, wisdom, loyalty, courtesy and filial piety, or the attitude of obedience. Chinese culture contains a big variation of music, dancing, painting, and arts.

The ancient written standard Chinese has been used for thousands of years and the Chinese written language is one of the most seminal aspects of its culture as it is very varied and artsy. The Chinese dialects are among the world’s most ancient languages. The biggest dialect is Standard Mandarin as it is spoken by circa one billion citizens. First the Chinese were writing on plates made of wood and later on they invented paper. And what's so special about the language is that they use the same alphabet that they used five thousand years ago.

Chinese culture went through lots of turning points, even though the culture was already rich and essential around the pre-china period. The pre-china period saw the formation of language with science and technology that they used with great intelligence and diversity. A big part of the culture is mostly about finding the balance between yin and yang and with yin and yang finding harmony. A larger part of Chinese culture is that it is based on a spiritual world, which means that they believe there is a spiritual world. Another big part of Chinese culture is the martial arts because china is the original birth place of eastern martial arts. The most common martial art is kung fu or first name wushu. The first generation of art's purpose is more about survival and warfare than art even though some art have retained a distinct Chinese flavor.
